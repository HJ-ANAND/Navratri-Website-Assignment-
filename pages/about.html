About Navratri

Navratri is a vibrant Hindu festival celebrated over nine nights in honor of Goddess Durga and her nine divine forms. It symbolizes the triumph of good over evil and is observed with devotion, fasting, prayers, and joyous cultural traditions. Each day of Navratri is dedicated to a different form of the goddess, reflecting unique virtues and powers. People decorate temples and homes, wear traditional attire, and participate in Garba and Dandiya Raas dances. Beyond spirituality, Navratri fosters community bonding, cultural expression, and festive joy, making it one of the most significant and colorful celebrations in India.
