Navratri – 9 Days of Celebration

Day 1 – Shailputri

Worshipped as the daughter of the mountains, Goddess Shailputri represents purity and strength. She rides a bull and carries a trident.

Day 2 – Brahmacharini

She symbolizes penance, devotion, and wisdom. Goddess Brahmacharini inspires us to walk the path of love and dedication.

Day 3 – Chandraghanta

Depicted with a crescent moon on her forehead, Chandraghanta represents courage and serenity, blessing devotees with bravery.

Day 4 – Kushmanda

Known as the creator of the universe, Goddess Kushmanda’s radiant energy illuminates all directions, bringing health and prosperity.

Day 5 – Skandamata

She holds baby Kartikeya in her lap and symbolizes motherly love and strength, protecting her devotees from harm.

Day 6 – Katyayani

Born to sage Katyayan, Goddess Katyayani is a fierce warrior form, representing the destroyer of evil forces.

Day 7 – Kalaratri

Fierce and dark, Kalaratri removes all negative energies and blesses her devotees with protection and courage.

Day 8 – Mahagauri

Goddess Mahagauri is a symbol of purity and tranquility, blessing devotees with calmness, wisdom, and fulfillment.

Day 9 – Siddhidatri

On the final day, Siddhidatri blesses devotees with supernatural powers and fulfills all divine aspirations.
